Help Me Decide

Making decisions easier, together

Problem Statement

Many people struggle with decision-making, especially when faced with multiple options and conflicting opinions. Current solutions like group chats and polls lack structure and make it difficult to track feedback, weigh different perspectives, and arrive at confident decisions.

Design Opportunity

How might we create a mobile app that helps users make better decisions by gathering structured input from trusted friends and family, while providing clear visual insights to guide their final choice?

Product Users

Young adults and professionals who frequently make decisions affecting their daily lives - from choosing restaurants and planning trips to making career moves and major purchases. They value input from friends and family but need a better way to organize and process that feedback.

Usability Testing

Conducted moderated usability tests with 8 participants to evaluate the poll creation and voting flows. Participants were asked to complete key tasks while thinking aloud.

Finding #1

Issue: Users were confused about how to set criteria weights

Solution: Added tooltips and a brief tutorial on first use explaining the weighted voting system

Finding #2

Issue: Some users wanted to see real-time results while voting was still open

Solution: Added a toggle option for poll creators to choose whether results are visible before voting closes

Finding #3

Issue: Users wanted more visual variety in how results were displayed

Solution: Implemented multiple chart types (bar, pie, comparison table) that users can toggle between

Limitations

While Help Me Decide addresses many pain points in collaborative decision-making, there are some constraints to acknowledge:

  • The app relies on having an engaged network - its value decreases if friends don't actively participate in polls
  • Very complex decisions with many nuanced factors may still require in-person discussion beyond what the app can facilitate
  • The weighted voting system, while powerful, requires a learning curve that some users may find initially overwhelming
  • Privacy concerns around decision-making data need ongoing attention and clear communication

These limitations inform future iterations and remind us that the app works best as a supplement to, not replacement for, meaningful conversations with trusted advisors.

Reflection

What I Learned

This project taught me the importance of balancing feature complexity with user experience simplicity. The biggest challenge was creating a weighted voting system that felt intuitive rather than overwhelming. Through multiple rounds of testing and iteration, I learned that progressive disclosure - revealing advanced features gradually - was key to maintaining simplicity while offering depth.

Future Iterations

If I were to iterate further, I would explore:

  • AI-powered suggestions based on past decisions and user preferences
  • Integration with calendar apps for time-sensitive decisions
  • A more robust social component to help users build trusted decision-making communities
  • Analytics showing users their decision-making patterns over time
  • Collaborative decision templates for common scenarios (vacation planning, restaurant selection, etc.)

Impact

The ultimate goal is to reduce decision fatigue and increase confidence in choices by providing structure and community input. Early user feedback suggests that the app successfully addresses the core problem of scattered, disorganized feedback that makes decision-making harder than it needs to be.
